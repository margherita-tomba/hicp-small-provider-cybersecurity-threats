# Top 5 Cyber Threats to a Small Healthcare Provider

## Overview
This project analyzes the top cybersecurity threats affecting small healthcare providers based on the HHS 405(d) Health Industry Cybersecurity Practices (HICP) framework.

The objective is to translate healthcare cybersecurity guidance into practical and realistic security measures for small clinics and outpatient healthcare organizations.

---

## Why This Matters
Cyberattacks against healthcare organizations can disrupt patient care, expose sensitive health information, and affect patient safety.

Small healthcare providers are particularly vulnerable because they often operate with limited IT and cybersecurity resources.

---

## Example Healthcare Organization
This project assumes a fictional outpatient healthcare clinic with:

- 25 employees
- cloud-based EHR system
- connected medical devices
- outsourced IT support
- limited cybersecurity budget

---

## Top 5 Cybersecurity Threats

| Threat | Healthcare Impact | Practical Mitigations |
|---|---|---|
| Social Engineering | Credential theft and unauthorized EHR access | MFA, phishing awareness training, email filtering |
| Ransomware | Disrupted care and inaccessible patient records | Offline backups, patching, endpoint protection |
| Loss or Theft of Devices | Exposure of ePHI | Device encryption, remote wipe, screen locks |
| Insider Data Loss | Unauthorized disclosure of patient information | Least privilege, access reviews, audit logging |
| Medical Device Attacks | Disruption to patient care | Network segmentation, vulnerability management |
